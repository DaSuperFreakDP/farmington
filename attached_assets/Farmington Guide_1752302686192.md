## How to Play

Ok this ain’t your typical fantasy sporting event. This is is a fantasy farming tournament where players choose familiar faces to be a part of their farming team. Each matchday, the farmers will have to participate in a task based on their position (Ex. Fix Meiser is in charge of fixing things on the farm such as the fence). If he succeeds he gets points and if he fails, he doesn’t. However there are also crop yields that contribute to the points. You will face off against other players in your league to see who can get the most points in a three day match cycle. While the game relies on random number generation to see if you pass or fail, there is much strategy in how you choose your team, trade with others and win the season. Jump to any section of the almanac to get a better guide for specific parts of Farmington

## Match Stats Tab
In this tab you can see the day by day match stats for your team

The top area shows the total points accumulated this season sorted by your farmers

Next in the matchday history we can see the three starting farmers’ performances.

The way to read the performance is to simply look at the third **‘TOTAL’ column** which has the total points the farmer earned for that matchday.
If you want to get more technical here we go:

 - **The 'NAME' column** has the names of each farmer and the heart emoji means that farmer has the preference for the daily crop of that matchday (ex. Tyler’s preferred summer crop is peppers and that day’s crop was peppers)

-   **The job column** shows what position they were assigned to for that matchday
-   **task pts column** shows the total number of points that farmer earned from succeeding in their daily task. A farmer gets 1 point for passing the task and more points for how much more his stat was than the random roll (ex. If farmer has a 10 strength and the random number generated was 7 he should get 3 extra points and collect 4 task points total) if he failed his task, he gets none
-   **Crop pts column.** If your farmer succeeds in his task he can harvest 30-50 crops of the daily crops which is added to his total point count. If he failed his task he yields 5-20 crops. However there are 3 things that can affect their harvest amount 
1. *Farmer preferences*: if the farmer has the same preferred crop type as the daily crop they are harvesting, they will collect the crop with a **1.5x multiplier** (if the farmer originally pulled 30 crops, the 1.5 multiplier would increase your total crop yield to 45, a 15 point increase). 
2. *Injuries*: injuries will lead your farmer to have a **0.4x multiplier** on their crop yield (ex. 30 x 0.4 = 12). 
3. *Level 1 Catastrophes:* if your farmer encounters a level 1 catastrophe they will also have a **0.4x multiplier** applied to their harvest
-   These multipliers are stackable (ex. You can have an injury and a catastrophe occur which would lead to two 0.4x multipliers being applied.
-   The multiplier order of applying is preference -> catastrophe -> injury = final value

- **Catloss** is the amount of points you lost from catastrophes with the acronym “ABC” indicating your farmer who was “Affected By Catastrophe”. Catastrophe level 1 makes one farmer lose 1 point for minor disasters. Level two makes all farmers lose 2 points. And a level three catastrophe makes all farmers collect no points but is very rare

  

- **Injloss** is the total number of points you losses to injuries that matchday

  

- **Injtot** is the total number of injuries your farmer has had this season

  

- **Injpttotal** is the total number of points they have losses due to injuries this season

  

- **Avg pts** is their average total points across all matchdays this season

## My Farm Tab
In this tab you can reassign the positions of your farmers

there’s 3 positions:

  

**Fix meisers** are responsible for fixing the mishaps on the farm and creating things. Putting someone with a high **handiness* stat* in this position would be wise

  

**Lift tenders** are responsible for the heavy lifting on the farm. From haybales to moving dead vehicles. Putting someone with a high *strength* *stat* in this position is a must

  

**Speed runners** are responsible for completing tasks that require a lot of endurance and agility. They have to be pretty quick on their feet. Putting someone with a high *stamina* *stat* is important for this position.

  

*Physical health* - farmers with high *physical health stat* have a better chance of not getting injured. Basically means they’re durable

  

To rearrange your farmers click the drop down selection, select the name of the farmer u want to replace that farmer and then on the replacing farmers dropdown selector click the name of the farmer being replaced. Then apply the changes

## Leaderboard Tab

shows the ranking of players both in your league and on a global setting (in and outside of your league). It also shows the brackets and whether the league is in its playoff season.

## Farmer Stats Tab

shows all of the currently drafted farmers, their points, owner and more. You can sort from ascending and descending values to get a better understanding of better performing farmers in the league as well as looking at the difference between other players farmers for the same role as your farmers in the last column.

## Leagues Tab
Shows the league settings for the host and shows your current matchup opponent and 3-day matchday cycle statistics. Whoever has more points between you and you opponent at the end of the 3 days gets a win and the other gets a loss. The more wins you have will help you to win the league.

## How to Choose Farmers

**Stats**
You want to look for farmers with the best stats for the roles on your team. (ex. a farmer with a high strength stat will succeed more often in the lift tender's strength based tasks than a farmer with a lower strength stat).
Try to find 3 farmers that can perform well in the 3 starting positions. Meaning a farmer with a high strength stat, a farmer with a high handy stat, and a farmer with a high stamina stat for the Lift Tender, Fix Meiser and Speed Runner stats respectfully.

The Physical Stat is also important as players with a higher physical stat have a lesser chance to get injured. 

**Crop Preferences**
Right now there's two ways I can think of organizing your team based on crops. Crop preferences lead to 1.5x multipliers being added to your farmer's crop yield which means strategizing what crop preferences you want your farmers to have can be ideal. There are 6 total crop possibilities for each season. You have a 1 in 6 chance to have the crop you want each matchday. Here are some ideas on how to strategize your harvest.

 1. All the Same Preference
One idea is to try to organize your team so that they all have the same preference. This could lead to very high crop yields. If you have three farmers who prefer tomatoes and the daily crop is tomatoes each farmer would receive a 1.5x multiplier on their yield. (Ex. if each farmer scored 30 crops on that matchday, your team would have 45 points more at the end of the matchday which could make a huge difference to win against your opponents).

 2. Diversify 
 Another Idea is to diversify your crop preferences so that all of your farmers have difference crop preferences so that their is a higher chance that one will activate the multiply. (Ex. One farmer who prefers tomatoes, one who prefers watermelon, and one who prefers corn)

**Check their performance**
if you are unsure about the stats and preferences, take a look at the farmers average performance and averages of farmers on other teams. If you like what you see, take it (through trading)
